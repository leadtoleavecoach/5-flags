The Leadership Gap

Why your business still runs through you

If you’re the only one who can really make a decision in your business, you don’t have a staffing problem — you have a leadership gap. The good news: it’s fixable, and it’s almost always one of five specific things. Here’s how to spot each one — and the first move to close it.
1
Flag One
Everything routes through you

The Flag: Your team texts you for answers they could figure out themselves. You’re the bottleneck, and nothing moves until you weigh in.

The Rep: Before you answer, ask “What would you do?” Make them bring a solution, not just a problem. You’re training decision-making one question at a time.

2
Flag Two
Your managers manage tasks, not people

The Flag: They assign work and check boxes, but they don’t coach, develop, or have the hard conversations. They were promoted for being great at the job — not for leading.

The Rep: Give each manager one person to actively develop this month. Leadership is grown through reps, not titles.

3
Flag Three
Nobody owns the outcome

The Flag: When something goes wrong, you hear excuses and finger-pointing instead of ownership.

The Rep: Assign clear owners to outcomes, not just tasks. Replace “whose fault was it?” with “what will you do differently?” Watch the culture shift.

4
Flag Four
Feedback only comes from you

The Flag: You’re the only one who corrects, praises, or redirects. The whole team waits for your read on everything.

The Rep: Teach your managers one simple feedback script they can use daily — so course-correction happens without you in the room.

5
Flag Five
The team freezes when you’re gone

The Flag: Vacations aren’t restful because things stall or break the moment you step away.

The Rep: Run a “what would you do if I were unreachable?” drill. Whatever they can’t answer is your leadership to-do list.
